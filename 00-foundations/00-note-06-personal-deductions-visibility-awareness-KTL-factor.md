## Summary

At the core of all marketing — regardless of channel, industry, or format — are two non-negotiable objectives: **visibility** (being seen) and **brand awareness** (being known). Before any consumer parts with their money, they need to know, trust, and like what they are buying into. Marketing is the ongoing process of building that know-trust-like factor high enough that a purchase becomes the natural next step. Different digital marketing channels each contribute a distinct piece of this puzzle — and the most powerful signals of marketing success are not just sales, but repeat purchases and referrals, because both require the customer to stake something of their own.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Visibility** — being seen; the prerequisite for everything else
- **Brand Awareness** — being known; reputation built over time through consistent presence
- **The Know-Trust-Like Factor** — the three conditions a consumer must meet before buying
- **Each channel serves a different function** in building know-trust-like
- **Repeat purchases = satisfaction** — the customer liked the experience enough to return
- **Referrals = deep trust** — the customer is willing to stake their own reputation on yours

## Details & Deep Dives

### The Two Foundational Marketing Objectives

No matter what is being marketed — a brand, a product, a service, a person, an organisation — two things must be achieved before anything else can work:

**1. Visibility — Being Seen**
Nothing happens if no one knows you exist. Visibility is the entry point. It is about showing up in the spaces where your target audience already is, consistently enough that they begin to notice you.

**2. Brand Awareness — Being Known**
Visibility gets you noticed once. Brand awareness is what happens when people notice you repeatedly — it builds recognition, familiarity, and reputation over time. Being known means people have a sense of who you are, what you stand for, and what to expect from you.

> Visibility is a moment. Brand awareness is a relationship with time.

---

### The Know-Trust-Like Factor

Before a consumer parts with their money, three conditions must be met:

| Condition | What It Means | How It's Built |
|---|---|---|
| **Know** | They are aware you exist and understand what you offer | Visibility, brand awareness, clear messaging |
| **Trust** | They believe you can deliver what you promise | Consistency, proof, authority, transparency |
| **Like** | They feel positively toward you — your values, voice, and personality resonate | Authentic communication, relatability, shared values |

All three must be present. A brand can be highly visible but not trusted — and no sale happens. A brand can be liked but unknown — and no sale happens. Marketing's job is to build all three simultaneously and keep them high.

> People do not part with their wealth on a whim. They invest in an experience they believe will deliver a transformation — and they need to know, trust, and like the source of that experience before they will take that risk.

---

### What Repeat Purchases and Referrals Actually Signal

The most powerful indicators of marketing success are not just conversion rates — they are what happens *after* the sale.

**Repeat Purchase**
When a customer comes back, they are signalling:
- The experience delivered on its promise
- The transformation was real
- The know-trust-like factor is strong enough to survive post-purchase reality

**Referral**
When a customer refers someone else, they are doing something far more significant — they are putting their *own* reputation on the line. A referral says:
> *"I believe in this enough to attach my name to it in front of someone I care about."*

This is the highest form of marketing validation — and it cannot be manufactured. It is earned through consistent delivery of a genuine experience.

> Affiliates and influencers are essentially formalised referrals. They are people who have decided — or been positioned — to stake their reputation on a brand. The most effective ones genuinely believe in what they promote. The ones who don't eventually lose the trust of their audience.

### How Each Digital Marketing Channel Contributes

Different channels serve different functions in building the know-trust-like factor:

| Channel | Primary Contribution | Know-Trust-Like Function |
|---|---|---|
| **SEO** | Being found when someone is actively searching | Know — puts you in front of people looking for solutions |
| **Content Marketing** | Providing value, establishing authority | Trust + Know — demonstrates expertise over time |
| **Social Media Marketing** | Boosting visibility and engagement | Visibility + Like — personality and presence |
| **Email Marketing** | Nurturing leads toward conversion | Trust + Like — consistent, personal communication |
| **PPC Advertising** | Driving immediate targeted traffic | Visibility — fast reach to a defined audience |
| **Influencer Marketing** | Borrowed trust through a known personality | Trust + Know — credibility transferred from influencer to brand |
| **Affiliate Marketing** | Extended reach through aligned partners | Trust — affiliates vouch for the brand to their audience |
| **Attraction Marketing** | Magnetic, value-first presence | All three — know, trust, and like built simultaneously |

No single channel builds all three conditions equally well. A complete digital marketing strategy combines channels deliberately to cover all three.

## Actionable Takeaways

- When evaluating any marketing channel or tactic, ask: *which part of know-trust-like does this serve?*
- Never sacrifice trust for visibility — being seen by everyone means nothing if what they see erodes confidence
- Track repeat purchase rate and referral activity as key marketing health metrics — not just conversion
- Build your personal brand with all three in mind: show up consistently (know), deliver real value (trust), be genuinely human (like)
