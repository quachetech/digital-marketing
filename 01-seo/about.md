# 01 · Search Engine Optimisation (SEO)

---

## What Is This?

SEO is the practice of making your content findable on search engines — primarily Google — without paying for placement. When someone types a question into Google, SEO determines whether your page shows up, and where.

It works by understanding how search engines crawl, index, and rank content, then creating and structuring content in a way that earns visibility. It's part technical, part creative, part psychology — because at its core, SEO is about understanding what people are searching for and giving them the best possible answer.

---

## Why It Matters

Organic search is one of the most powerful and sustainable sources of traffic. Unlike paid ads, SEO compounds over time — content you create today can bring in traffic for years. For someone building a personal brand or selling courses, SEO is how people who've never heard of you find you.

## Key Tools & Platforms

- **Google Search Console** — monitor search performance
- **Google Keyword Planner** — keyword research (free)
- **Ubersuggest / Ahrefs / Semrush** — keyword & competitor research
- **Yoast SEO** — on-page SEO plugin (if using WordPress)
- **PageSpeed Insights** — technical performance

---

## Key Terms

- **SERP** — Search Engine Results Page; the page of results Google shows
- **Keyword** — a word or phrase people type into search engines
- **Search Intent** — the reason behind a search query (informational, navigational, transactional)
- **On-Page SEO** — optimisations made directly on your webpage
- **Off-Page SEO** — signals from outside your site (backlinks, mentions)
- **Technical SEO** — the infrastructure that makes your site crawlable and fast
- **Backlink** — a link from another website pointing to yours
- **Domain Authority** — a metric estimating how trustworthy/authoritative your site is
- **Meta Description** — the short summary shown under your page title in search results
- **Crawling / Indexing** — how search engines discover and store your content
