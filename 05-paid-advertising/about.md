# 05 · Paid Advertising

---

## What Is This?

Paid advertising is paying platforms — Google, Meta, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, and others — to show your content or offers to a targeted audience. Unlike organic strategies that compound slowly, paid ads can generate traffic and results almost immediately.

It operates on auction-based systems where you bid for visibility, and the platforms use algorithms to match your ads to the most relevant audiences based on targeting parameters you set.

---

## Why It Matters

Organic reach takes time. Paid advertising gives you a tap you can turn on when you need traffic, leads, or sales now. For course launches, product promotions, and audience building, paid ads are often the accelerant. Understanding them also makes you a sharper marketer overall — because spending money focuses the mind on what actually converts.

## Key Terms

- **PPC (Pay-Per-Click)** — you pay each time someone clicks your ad
- **CPM (Cost Per Mille)** — cost per 1,000 impressions
- **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)** — cost to acquire one customer or conversion
- **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** — revenue earned per dollar/rand spent on ads
- **Impression** — one instance of your ad being shown
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** — percentage of people who saw the ad and clicked it
- **Ad Creative** — the visual and written content of the ad itself
- **Targeting** — defining who sees your ad based on demographics, interests, behaviour
- **Retargeting** — showing ads to people who have already interacted with your brand
- **Landing Page** — the page someone arrives on after clicking your ad
- **Ad Fatigue** — when an audience sees your ad so often they stop responding to it
