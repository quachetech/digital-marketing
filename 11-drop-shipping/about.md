# 11 · Dropshipping

---

## What Is This?

Dropshipping is an e-commerce model where you sell physical products online without ever holding inventory. When a customer places an order in your store, you purchase the item from a third-party supplier who ships it directly to the customer. You never touch the product.

Your margin is the difference between what the customer pays you and what you pay the supplier. Your work is in building and running the store, finding winning products, and — critically — marketing them.

---

## Why It Matters

Dropshipping is one of the most commonly taught "start a business online" models, and understanding it is relevant in two ways: as a potential income stream in its own right, and as a practical case study in e-commerce marketing. Running a dropshipping store forces you to apply paid advertising, product page copywriting, conversion optimisation, and customer psychology all at once. It's a serious marketing education in a live environment.

## Key Tools & Platforms

- **Shopify** — most popular dropshipping store platform
- **WooCommerce** — WordPress-based alternative
- **AliExpress / Zendrop / Spocket / CJDropshipping** — product sourcing
- **DSers / AutoDS** — order automation and management
- **Meta Ads / TikTok Ads** — primary paid traffic sources for dropshipping
- **Google Trends / Minea / AdSpy** — product and ad research

---

## Key Terms

- **Dropshipping** — selling products online without holding inventory; supplier ships directly to customer
- **Supplier** — the manufacturer or wholesaler who fulfils your orders
- **Fulfilment** — the process of processing and delivering an order to the customer
- **Profit Margin** — the difference between what you charge the customer and what you pay the supplier
- **SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)** — a unique identifier for each product variant
- **AOV (Average Order Value)** — the average amount spent per transaction in your store
- **Conversion Rate** — percentage of store visitors who complete a purchase
- **Product-Market Fit** — when a product genuinely meets a demand in a specific market
- **Winning Product** — a product with proven demand, good margins, and strong ad potential
- **Chargeback** — when a customer disputes a charge and the payment is reversed
